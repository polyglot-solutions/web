Programa Parole in Place (PIP)

¿Usted o un ser querido vive actualmente en el ¿Estados Unidos sin estatus legal? El nuevo Programa de libertad condicional, anunciado el El 18 de junio de 2024 ofrece una extraordinaria oportunidad de quedarse, trabajar y vivir legalmente en EE.UU. Estamos listos para proporcionar la soporte, y actualizaciones que necesitas realizar correctamente transición al estatus de residente permanente en el Estados Unidos.

¿Quien es elegible?

Cónyuges e hijos de ciudadanos estadounidenses
- Debe haber vivido en los EE. UU. continuamente durante al menos 10 años.
- Entrada a los EE.UU. ocurrió sin permiso oficial y usted no han partido durante todo el período de 10 años.
- Prueba de matrimonio con un ciudadano estadounidense o prueba de ser hijo (incluidos hijastros) de una Se requiere ciudadano estadounidense.
- No descalificante delitos.

College-educated DACA Recipients and Dreamers
- Must have a degree from an accredited U.S. institution of higher education.
- Must have a job offer from a U.S. employer in a field related to their degree.

Service Members and Families
- Includes active-duty members of the U.S. armed forces or individuals in the Selected Reserve of the Ready Reserve.
- Includes spouses, widow(er)s, parents, sons, or daughters of eligible service members.
- Does not require 10 years of residency in the U.S. but provides parole in 1-year increments, renewable.

How the Program Works?

The Parole in Place program is expected to be structured in two key steps:
1. File an Application for Eligibility. Submit necessary documents to be considered for parole status.
2. Apply for Permanent Residence. Once granted parole, you will file your application for permanent residence, including applications for continued work permission and possibly a travel parole.

When Can You Apply?

The official rules and application forms for the Parole in Place program are anticipated to be announced soon. It is crucial to wait for the government to release clear guidelines and forms in the Federal Register before submitting any applications. Attempting to apply prematurely could result in your application being rejected.
